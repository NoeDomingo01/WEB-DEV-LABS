Other Popular Esports Games

While games like Dota 2, League of Legends, Valorant, CS:GO, and MLBB dominate the scene, there are many other esports titles that have made significant contributions to competitive gaming. These games appeal to different audiences and show the variety of esports genres worldwide.

Fortnite

Fortnite, developed by Epic Games, is a battle royale game that exploded in popularity due to its building mechanics, vibrant graphics, and large-scale tournaments. Its World Cup offered one of the biggest prize pools in esports history.

Overwatch

Blizzard’s Overwatch is a team-based hero shooter that combines FPS gameplay with unique abilities. The Overwatch League (OWL) was one of the first franchised esports leagues, bringing city-based teams into the competitive spotlight.

Apex Legends

Respawn Entertainment’s Apex Legends is another battle royale that gained massive popularity for its fast-paced gameplay, hero characters, and exciting tournaments. It has established itself as a serious contender in the battle royale esports genre.

PUBG

PlayerUnknown’s Battlegrounds (PUBG) is considered the game that popularized the battle royale genre. With both PC and mobile versions, PUBG has become a global esports hit, especially in Asia.

Rocket League

Rocket League mixes soccer with rocket-powered cars, creating one of the most unique esports experiences. The Rocket League Championship Series (RLCS) continues to attract players and fans from all over the world.

These titles showcase the diversity of esports, proving that competitive gaming isn’t limited to just one genre or platform.
