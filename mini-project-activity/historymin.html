The History of Dota 2

The story of Dota 2 begins with the original Defense of the Ancients (DotA), a custom map created for Warcraft III: Reign of Chaos and its expansion The Frozen Throne. The map, developed by the community, quickly became one of the most popular mods in gaming history. It introduced the concept of a Multiplayer Online Battle Arena (MOBA), where teams of heroes fought in strategic battles to control the map and destroy the enemy base.

Seeing its potential, Valve hired IceFrog, one of the lead developers of DotA, to create a standalone sequel. This led to the release of Dota 2 in 2013 on Steam. Unlike the mod, Dota 2 was built on Valve’s Source engine, which allowed for modern graphics, matchmaking, and continuous updates.

From the beginning, Dota 2 embraced esports. Valve introduced The International in 2011, even before the game’s full release, with an unprecedented prize pool of $1.6 million. This event set the tone for Dota 2 as one of the most competitive and community-driven games ever. Over the years, The International became legendary, breaking records with prize pools exceeding $40 million, all crowdfunded by players through the in-game Battle Pass.
