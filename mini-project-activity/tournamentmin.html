Major Dota 2 Tournaments

Competitive Dota 2 thrives on its tournaments, which showcase the best teams in the world and attract millions of fans. The most famous of these is The International (TI), an annual championship organized by Valve. First held in 2011, TI set a new standard for esports events by offering a massive $1.6 million prize pool. Since then, it has grown into the largest esports tournament, with prize pools exceeding $40 million, funded by the community through in-game purchases.

In addition to TI, Valve also organizes the Dota Pro Circuit (DPC), a seasonal league system where teams compete across regions to earn points and qualify for The International. This ensures a highly competitive environment throughout the year, keeping fans engaged and teams motivated.
