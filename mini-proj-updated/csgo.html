Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO)

One of the most iconic tactical FPS esports in history.

About CS:GO

Counter-Strike: Global Offensive (CS:GO), released by Valve in 2012, became one of the most iconic esports titles in history. Built on the legendary Counter-Strike franchise, CS:GO evolved into a competitive battleground emphasizing teamwork, map control, tactical execution, and precise mechanical aim. The game features bomb-planting and hostage-rescue modes, where two teams — Terrorists and Counter-Terrorists — must outsmart and outshoot each other in round-based competition.

CS:GO built a decade-long legacy with the Majors, some of the most prestigious tournaments in esports. Iconic orgs such as Natus Vincere (Na’Vi), Astralis, Fnatic, and Team Liquid helped shape competitive FPS play. Astralis in particular became known for pioneering tactical utility usage and structured team coordination, setting new standards in esports professionalism.

Legacy & Competitive Scene

CS:GO shaped esports culture through LAN tournaments, open qualifiers, and strong community support. The pro scene grew through circuits such as ESL, BLAST Premier, and Valve Majors, creating legendary rivalries and unforgettable moments. In 2023, the franchise evolved into Counter-Strike 2, continuing the competitive legacy with updated technology while preserving the core tactical gameplay fans love.

  • High skill ceiling and strategic depth
  • Legendary teams and historic esports rivalries
  • Long-standing community and player-driven ecosystem
  • Foundation for modern tactical shooters worldwide
