Early Beginnings (1970s ‚Äì 1990s)

Esports began in the early 1970s when the first competitive video game tournament was held at Stanford University. Games like Spacewar and the rise of arcade titles such as PAC-MAN and Street Fighter introduced friendly competition among players.

Rise of Online Gaming (2000s)

With faster internet, competitive gaming moved online. Titles such as StarCraft in South Korea, Counter-Strike, and DOTA helped establish professional gaming leagues and global tournaments. Live streaming and LAN events became popular.

Modern Esports Era (2010s ‚Äì Today)

Esports exploded into mainstream culture through games like Dota 2, League of Legends, Valorant, Fortnite, and Mobile Legends. Major arenas, million-dollar prize pools, and official pro leagues now define the industry.

Esports in the Philippines

The Philippines has become a strong esports nation, especially in Mobile Legends and Valorant. Filipino teams like Bren Esports and Blacklist International gained global recognition, bringing pride to the country.
